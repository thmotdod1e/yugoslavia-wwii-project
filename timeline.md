# Timeline of UK-Yugoslavia Relations in WWII

## March 1941
- **March 25, 1941**: Yugoslav government signs the Tripartite Pact under pressure from Germany.

## April 1941
- **April 6, 1941**: Axis invasion of Yugoslavia begins with bombing of Belgrade.
- **April 17, 1941**: Yugoslavia surrenders; country partitioned among Axis powers.

## June 1941
- **June 1941**: Formation of Chetnik resistance movement under Colonel Draža Mihailović, initially recognized by the Yugoslav government-in-exile and the Allies.

## November 1942
- **November 1942**: Teheran Conference - Allied leaders discuss support for Yugoslav resistance factions.

## 1943
- *Entry to be added: British decision to switch support from Chetniks to Partisans.*

## 1944
- **June 1944**: Operation Rösselsprung - German airborne attempt to capture Tito at Drvar.
- **October 1944**: Belgrade Offensive - Soviet and Partisan forces liberate Belgrade.

## 1945
- **May 1945**: End of WWII in Europe; Yugoslavia under communist rule.
